# Module 6, Lesson 4: Automation Consulting

## The Highest Value Model

Consulting delivers the highest income per hour of any zero-cost business model. You're not selling products or content—you're selling your expertise, strategic thinking, and problem-solving abilities.

Businesses are desperate for guidance on AI automation. They know they need it, they don't know how to do it, and they're willing to pay well for someone who can help them figure it out.

## What Automation Consultants Do

### Assessment and Strategy

**What It Is:**
Evaluate a business's current processes, identify automation opportunities, and create a roadmap for implementation.

**Deliverables:**
- Process audit report
- Automation opportunity assessment
- ROI projections
- Implementation roadmap
- Tool recommendations

**Pricing:** $1,500-$10,000 per engagement

**Time Required:** 5-20 hours

### Implementation Guidance

**What It Is:**
Guide a business through the implementation of automation, providing expertise while their team does the work.

**Deliverables:**
- Implementation plan
- Weekly guidance calls
- Troubleshooting support
- Progress reviews
- Optimization recommendations

**Pricing:** $200-$500/hour or $3,000-$10,000/month retainer

**Time Required:** 5-15 hours/week

### Done-With-You Implementation

**What It Is:**
Work alongside the business's team to implement automation, teaching while doing.

**Deliverables:**
- Hands-on implementation
- Team training
- Documentation
- Knowledge transfer
- Ongoing support

**Pricing:** $5,000-$25,000 per project

**Time Required:** 20-60 hours

### Training and Workshops

**What It Is:**
Teach teams how to implement and manage their own automation.

**Deliverables:**
- Workshop curriculum
- Training sessions
- Hands-on exercises
- Reference materials
- Follow-up support

**Pricing:** $2,000-$10,000 per workshop or $500-$2,000/hour for training

**Time Required:** 4-16 hours per workshop

## Positioning Yourself as a Consultant

### Define Your Niche

Don't be a general "AI consultant." Be specific:

**Too Broad:**
"I help businesses with AI automation."

**Better:**
"I help small businesses automate their operations."

**Best:**
"I help 10-50 person professional service firms automate client onboarding and reduce administrative time by 40%."

The more specific, the easier to:
- Target marketing
- Command premium pricing
- Develop expertise
- Generate referrals

### Possible Niches

**By Industry:**
- Real estate agencies
- Law firms
- Accounting firms
- Marketing agencies
- Healthcare practices
- E-commerce businesses
- Manufacturing companies

**By Function:**
- Client onboarding
- Sales and CRM
- Marketing automation
- Operations streamlining
- Customer service automation
- Financial processes

**By Size:**
- Solopreneurs
- Small businesses (5-20 people)
- Growing businesses (20-100 people)
- Mid-size companies (100-500 people)

### Develop Your Methodology

Create a repeatable process that delivers results:

**Example: The Automation Roadmap Method**

1. **Discovery (Week 1)**
   - Stakeholder interviews
   - Process documentation
   - Pain point identification
   - Quick win identification

2. **Assessment (Week 2)**
   - Current state analysis
   - Automation opportunity scoring
   - ROI calculations
   - Tool evaluation

3. **Strategy (Week 3)**
   - Roadmap development
   - Priority recommendations
   - Implementation timeline
   - Resource requirements

4. **Presentation (Week 4)**
   - Executive presentation
   - Documentation delivery
   - Q&A session
   - Next steps discussion

A methodology makes you look professional, ensures consistent results, and justifies your pricing.

### Build Your Authority

**As you get clients:**

### Qualification

Not every lead is a good client. Qualify them:

**Good Fit:**
- Has budget for consulting
- Understands the value
- Ready to take action
- Decision-maker involved
- Realistic expectations

**Bad Fit:**
- Wants everything for free
- Doesn't see the value
- Not ready to implement
- Can't make decisions
- Expects overnight transformation

**Qualification Questions:**
- "What's your timeline for implementing automation?"
- "What budget have you allocated for this?"
- "Who will be involved in the decision?"
- "What have you tried before?"
- "What would success look like?"

### The Discovery Call

**Goal:** Understand their needs and determine fit.

**Structure:**
1. **Rapport (5 min)**
   - Introduction
   - How they found you
   - What prompted the call

2. **Exploration (15-20 min)**
   - Current situation
   - Pain points
   - Goals and timeline
   - Previous attempts
   - Budget and resources

3. **Value Preview (5 min)**
   - Share relevant insights
   - Mention similar situations
   - Preview how you'd help

4. **Next Steps (5 min)**
   - Propose the engagement
   - Explain your process
   - Discuss investment
   - Schedule follow-up

**Key Insight:**
The discovery call is not about selling—it's about understanding and qualifying. If there's a fit, selling happens naturally.

### The Proposal

**Structure:**

1. **Executive Summary**
   - Their situation in your words
   - The opportunity you see
   - Your recommendation

2. **Current State**
   - What you observed
   - Pain points identified
   - Opportunities spotted

3. **Proposed Solution**
   - Your methodology
   - Specific activities
   - Timeline
   - Deliverables

4. **Expected Outcomes**
   - Time savings projected
   - Cost savings projected
   - Efficiency improvements
   - ROI calculation

5. **Investment**
   - Fee structure
   - Payment terms
   - What's included

6. **Next Steps**
   - How to proceed
   - Timeline for decision
   - Contact information

**Pricing Psychology:**
- Present one option (easier decision)
- Or three options (give choice, anchor high)
- Include ROI justification
- Be confident in your pricing

### Closing

**Natural Close:**
If the proposal is right for them, closing should be natural:

"Based on what we discussed, I recommend the Assessment and Roadmap engagement. That's $5,000, and we can start next week. Does that work for you?"

**Objection Handling:**

**"It's too expensive."**
"I understand budget is a concern. Let's look at the ROI. Based on what you've told me, you're spending X hours per week on [manual process]. At your hourly rate, that's $Y per month. This system will save most of that time, paying for itself in Z months. After that, it's all profit. Would it help to see those numbers broken down?"

**"I need to think about it."**
"Of course. What specific questions do you have? I want to make sure you have all the information you need to make the right decision."

**"I need to talk to my partner."**
"Absolutely. Would it be helpful if we scheduled a brief call with them? I could answer any questions they have."

**"Can you do it for less?"**
"I appreciate you asking. The investment reflects the value and results you'll receive. What I can do is break it into phases so you can see results before committing to the full engagement. Would that help?"

## Pricing Your Services

### Hourly vs. Project vs. Retainer

**Hourly:**
- Good for: Uncertain scope, ongoing advisory
- Range: $150-$500/hour
- Pros: Simple, flexible
- Cons: Caps income, penalizes efficiency

**Project:**
- Good for: Defined scope, clear deliverables
- Range: $2,000-$25,000/project
- Pros: Value-based, predictable
- Cons: Scope creep risk, requires clarity

**Retainer:**
- Good for: Ongoing relationships, advisory
- Range: $2,000-$10,000/month
- Pros: Predictable income, deeper relationships
- Cons: Can become undervalued, requires management

### Recommended Approach

**Start with project pricing:**
- Easier to sell
- Clear deliverables
- Build portfolio

**Move to retainers:**
- After successful projects
- For ongoing relationships
- Creates predictable income

**Hourly only for:**
- Uncertain scope
- Quick consultations
- Existing relationships

### Value-Based Pricing

Price based on the value you create, not the time you spend:

**Example:**
A client spends 20 hours/week on manual processes.
Their time is worth $100/hour.
That's $2,000/week or $8,000/month in labor cost.

If you can automate 50% of that:
You save them $4,000/month or $48,000/year.

A $10,000 engagement that delivers this has a 5x ROI in year one.

This is why automation consulting commands premium prices—the value is clear and substantial.
